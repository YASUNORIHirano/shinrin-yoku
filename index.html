Shinrin-yoku in the Heart of Tokyo
~Five Senses, Gently Reawakened~

Tokyo can be overwhelming. I didn’t realize how tense I had become until I stepped into Rinshinomori Park. The first thing I noticed was the air.

A Place You Don’t Have to Travel Far For

I used to think I had to leave Tokyo to feel this kind of calm. But Rinshinomori Park is only about a 10-minute walk from Tokyu Musashi-Koyama Station. You don’t need a whole day. Even 30 to 60 minutes can feel like a small escape. The park stretches across Meguro and Shinagawa wards. It was once an experimental forestry site, but today it feels like a quiet refuge hidden within the city.

Smell — The Scent That Makes You Breathe

When I slow down and take a deep breath, the air changes. It carries a faint scent of trees and damp earth — cool, quiet, almost sweet.

I like to sit near the small bridge in the center of the park. With the pond beside me and trees overhead, I find myself breathing more deeply than I realize.

Some studies suggest forest air and phytoncides may support relaxation.　But when I'm here, I don’t think about research. I just breathe.

Sight — Light Through the Leaves

In spring, I walk beneath the cherry blossoms.

In early summer, the fresh green leaves feel almost luminous. Sunlight filters softly through the branches, creating gentle layers of green.

Even in winter, the bare trees hold their own quiet beauty. Looking up, I notice how kind the light feels on my eyes.

Hearing — The Silence That Sharpens

When I stop walking and simply listen, I notice how quiet it is

There are no artificial sounds. No traffic. Just the faint rustle of leaves and distant birds.

You might even hear something unexpected — your own breathing.

Touch — The Softness Underfoot

I didn’t expect the ground to feel so soft.

When I walk over fallen leaves, my steps slow down naturally. It feels as if the earth is gently supporting me.

I can almost hear the joyous cries of tired feet, used to hard asphalt, now resting on something kinder.

Taste — A Simple Pause

After walking for a while, I sometimes stop at a nearby café. A warm drink tastes different after time among the trees. Simple. Calm. Enough.

Before You Go

  • Location: Rinshinomori Park, Meguro & Shinagawa, Tokyo
  • Access: About a 10-minute walk from Tokyu Musashi-Koyama Station
  • Admission: Free
  • Best time: Early morning or late afternoon for quieter moments

A Small Invitation

Modern life demands constant attention — especially from our eyes and minds. In this park, I feel that demand soften. If you find yourself needing a pause during your time in Tokyo, this quiet forest might be waiting for you.
